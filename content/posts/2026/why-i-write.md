There is something irreplaceable about having your own corner of the internet.

## The Problem with Social Media

Social media platforms optimize for engagement, not for your long-term interests. Your posts are ephemeral, buried under algorithmic noise, and ultimately owned by a corporation that can delete your account at any time.

A personal blog is:

- **Yours** — the content lives at your domain, not on someone else's servers
- **Permanent** — posts from 10 years ago are still findable
- **Searchable** — Google indexes your writing, helping people who actually need it

## Writing as Thinking

The act of writing forces clarity. When I write a post explaining a technical concept, I inevitably discover gaps in my own understanding. The blank page is the best debugger.

> "Writing is thinking. To write well is to think clearly." — David McCullough

## What I Plan to Write About

This blog will cover:

1. **AI and Machine Learning** — practical experiments, observations, and opinions
2. **Software Engineering** — architecture decisions, tools, and lessons learned
3. **Career and Learning** — how to grow as a technical professional
